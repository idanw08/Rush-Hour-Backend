Instructions - Rush-hour

In this task you will play a version of the game Rush-hour.

Rush Hour is a sliding block puzzle invented by Nob Yoshigahara in the 1970s.

The Goal of The Game:

The main goal is to get the Red car out through the exit of the board by moving the other vehicles out of its way.
However, the cars and trucks obstruct the path which makes the puzzle harder.

The Game Board

The game board is a 6x6 grid, including cars and trucks, each colored differently.
Both cars and trucks are one square wide, cars are two squares long and trucks are three squares long.

Stages and Tasks

Next, you will be asked to solve 3 different Rush Hour puzzles, followed by a “game follow-up questionnaire”, which includes additional questions regarding your performance, and predictions about other participants’ performances.

A move is an action of moving one vehicle to an adjacent location on the board.

A correct solution is a sequence of moves that results in the red car passing through the exit route.

An optimal solution: a correct solution with a minimal number of moves.

A solution’s quality is determined by its correctness and the number of moves.

You will have 10 minutes to solve each Rush Hour puzzle.

The game ends when the time limit expires, or if you managed to solve it (the red car passing through the exit route) beforehand.

Your final solution is the sequence of moves taken to reach the state of the board at the end of the game.

Your performance quality will be assessed based on:
  1. Your final solution quality (including whether or not you have successfully managed to solve the puzzle before the time limit expired).
  2. Your answers to the game follow-up questionnaire.

Bonus

You will have the opportunity to receive up to 5$ bonus payment, based on your performance, which will be evaluated independently, as well as relative to other participants.

Buttons:

Undo

Cancels the last action taken.

Reset

Restores the initial state of the board (the car and the trucks will return to their first position).
